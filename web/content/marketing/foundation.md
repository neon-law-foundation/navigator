## A 501(c)(3) focused on access to justice

The **Neon Law Foundation** is a separate nonprofit that runs two programs, and together they *are* what access to
justice means here — not a price: an open-source codebase anyone can read and run, and education outreach with legal-aid
centers and state bar associations.

It is funded by donations and grants, governed independently of the Neon Law firm, and treats its work product as a
public good.

## The fight for access to justice

This is the fight we publish open-source code to win. The Legal Services Corporation's 2022 Justice Gap Report found
that **92% of civil legal problems** faced by low-income Americans received inadequate or no legal help. Millions of
people are taking on evictions, custody disputes, and debt collection without counsel — and winning is harder than it
has to be.

The World Justice Project's 2023 Rule of Law Index put the global number at **5.1 billion people** without meaningful
access to justice. AI and automation, in the hands of attorneys who know how to wield them, can put real representation
back within reach.

## Programs

- **Neon Law Navigator — open legal complement.**
  Neon Law Navigator is an open standard for combining templates, questionnaires, and workflows into an executable
  notation that spans entities and people.
- **CLE programs with bar associations.**
  Continuing legal education delivered in partnership with the Nevada State Bar and other jurisdictions — hands-on
  courses on citation verification, AI-assisted research, and the professional-responsibility rules that govern both.

## Training attorneys to take on more fights

Our CLE programs equip legal professionals with AI and automation skills to serve more clients, reduce costs, and
deliver competent representation at scale — so that the rights on the page reach the people they belong to.

- **AI-Assisted Legal Research.**
  Train attorneys to use AI tools responsibly for research and drafting while meeting professional responsibility
  obligations.
- **Workflow Automation.**
  Teach legal professionals to automate repetitive tasks so they can focus on the complex work that requires human
  judgment.
- **Expanding Legal Aid Capacity.**
  Help legal aid organizations and pro bono attorneys serve more clients with the same resources through technology.

## The firm and the Foundation

The Neon Law Foundation (a Nevada 501(c)(3) governed by an independent board) publishes open-source software and runs
CLEs. It does not provide legal services or render legal advice — that is the firm's role, at [neonlaw.com](/).
Donations and grants fund the Foundation; client fees fund the firm; the two organizations share branding and mission
but not governance, finances, or legal duties.
