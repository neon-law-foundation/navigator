# An uncontested divorce, handled with care

When two people have already agreed to part ways and settled every term between them, the paperwork should not be the
hard part. Neon Law Newleaf is a flat-fee uncontested divorce: a licensed attorney prepares and files the dissolution so
you can close one chapter and start the next.

[[pricing]]

**How it works.** Tell us what you have agreed — how property and debts are divided, any support, and any arrangements
for children. A licensed attorney prepares the dissolution documents, you review and sign, and we file them with the
court and see the matter through to the decree.

**What $555 covers.** Preparing and filing your uncontested dissolution — one flat legal fee. The court's filing fees
and any service-of-process costs are passed through at cost on top of the flat fee.

**We represent both of you — with a conflict waiver.** Even an amicable divorce is legally adverse, so representing both
spouses is something the law allows only with your informed, written consent. You both sign a conflict waiver up front:
the Firm represents the two of you jointly, prepares only the dissolution you have already agreed on, cannot advocate
for either of you against the other, and keeps no secrets between you. If a real disagreement comes up — anything about
property, debts, support, or children — the matter is no longer uncontested, the Firm steps back from both of you, and
each of you should get your own counsel. You are each encouraged to consult independent counsel before signing the
waiver.

**Engagement letter governs.** A written engagement letter signed before we start states the exact scope, the $555 fee,
the conflict waiver you both sign, and how either of us can end the representation. Before that letter goes out we run a
conflict check against every current and former matter at the firm. If we cannot properly take your matter, we tell you
promptly and refer you to counsel who also use the Neon Law Navigator. There are no referral fees between Neon Law and
any firm we refer matters to.
