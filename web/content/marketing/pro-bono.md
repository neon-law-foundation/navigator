# Free legal help when you can't afford a lawyer

Access to justice is the reason this firm exists. When you cannot afford a lawyer, we still show up — with the [Neon Law
Foundation](/foundation) and partner legal-aid organizations, a licensed attorney takes on your matter without charging
an attorney's fee.

[[pricing]]

**How it works.** Pro bono runs through our legal-aid partners. To take a matter without an attorney's fee, we need a
**SOLA — a Statement of Legal Aid — from one of our legal-aid partners**, confirming you qualify for free legal help.
Bring that, sign a written pro bono engagement, and a licensed attorney works your matter, in conjunction with the Neon
Law Foundation.

**What "free" covers — and what it doesn't.** Free means we do not charge an attorney's fee for the matter we take on.
It does not mean the matter has no costs: court filing fees, service-of-process charges, and similar costs may still
apply, and in some matters a court can shift fees between the parties. Your written pro bono engagement spells out
exactly what we cover and what remains your responsibility.

**Who it's for.** People who cannot afford to pay for the legal help they need. Our legal-aid partners run the
eligibility check and issue the SOLA — so you do not have to prove your finances to us; you bring the partner's
statement.

**Getting a SOLA.** If you do not have a Statement of Legal Aid yet, tell us what you're facing and we will point you to
a partner legal-aid organization that can assess your eligibility and issue one.

## Ask for help

Email **[support@neonlaw.org](mailto:support@neonlaw.org)** with a short note about what you're facing. We respond
within one business day and point you to the right help.
