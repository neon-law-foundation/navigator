What do agentic workflows mean for lawyers? Claude Code, Codex, computer use, browser agents — how can lawyers use these
tools? What's your favorite harness? Orchestration flow? How do you mitigate hallucinations, provide proper context to
drive value, deliver effective advice with guardrails, and thrive at trial and in court with AI?

Everyone is pushing adoption — clients, GCs, firm management, and the companies we advise. Our business counterparts are
off to the races; a lot of engineers do not write much code by hand anymore. We have a duty of competence that keeps
pointing this direction, and we owe it to our clients to do it well.

So let's talk it through. Come to a private lounge near downtown Seattle, meet other lawyers in town, and trade real
stories and ideas: use cases, workflows, and how people are mitigating hallucination.

The format is loose on purpose. A couple of hours, thinking out loud together. Beginners and advanced users are both
welcome. Open floor: bring a workflow, a prompt, a tool, or a war story. Present it, get feedback, even demo it live. No
prep required; you can just come and listen.

We all have a filing to crank out, a deal to close, or a call to take, so hop in and out as you need. Food and light
refreshments. Come meet your local bar and help push our profession's AI fluency forward.

And stop by before the long weekend: catch a few AI fireworks before the real ones.

trade ideas on putting AI to work in practice. Every lawyer and legal professional is welcome, any jurisdiction, whether
you come to present or just to listen.

Thursday, July 2, 2026, 11am-3pm Pacific. Private lounge, 1920 4th Ave, downtown Seattle. Food and refreshments. Free;
RSVP so we can plan headcount. Pop in and out as needed; community members can present every hour to accommodate busy
